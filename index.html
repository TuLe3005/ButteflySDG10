About the project

SDG 10: Reduce inequality within and among countries

"Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere. We are caught in an inescapable network of mutuality, tied in a single garment of destiny. Whatever affects one directly, affects all indirectly."

— Martin Luther King Jr
Letter for Birmingham Jail, Birmingham, Alabama, on April 16, 1963.

Highlighting the interconnected nature of our communities, the quote by Martin Luther King Jr. strongly reflects the vision of the United Nations’s Sustainable Development Goal 10: Reduced Inequalities, which promotes fair access to opportunities, resources, and rights for everyone. Society cannot truly develop if prosperity is limited to only a small number of people while others remain disadvantaged. When inequality persists, it weakens the balance and progress of the entire community. Likewise, when opportunities are unequal, it indicates that the system is failing, not the individual. Therefore, to build a sustainable future, we must move beyond merely sympathizing with those who are disadvantaged and instead work toward creating systems where opportunities and resources are distributed more fairly.
